## 2. Eligible activities & equipment (what AFG funds)

AFG's stated purpose is to provide resources that equip and train emergency personnel to recognized standards, outfit responders with compliant PPE, retrofit/modify facilities to protect personnel from known health hazards, acquire emergency response vehicles, and implement health/wellness/resiliency programs.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/assistance-grants ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-nofo_05182026.pdf

### 2a. Operations and Safety
Covers firefighting and non-affiliated EMS equipment, personal protective gear, training, wellness/fitness, and facility modifications. Sub-categories (project areas):

- **Equipment** — firefighting/EMS operational equipment. FEMA discourages "laundry lists" of small/miscellaneous items.
- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)** — turnout gear, SCBA, etc.; age/condition of existing PPE is a key evaluation factor. **FY25 change:** SCBA is now allowable regardless of age.
- **Wellness & Fitness** — holistic firefighter wellness. **Priority I** activities: initial medical exams, job-related immunizations, annual medical fitness evaluations, a behavioral health program, and a cancer screening program. **Priority II** items (e.g., fitness equipment) require the department to already have — or be implementing — all five Priority I activities. **FY25 change:** Priority I wellness/fitness activities were elevated to high-priority status.
- **Training** — professional training aimed at compliance with recognized (e.g., NFPA) standards.
- **Modifications to Facilities** — e.g., sprinkler system or source-capture exhaust; cannot change the structure's footprint; typically requires an Environmental and Historic Preservation (EHP) review.

**FY25 eligibility changes:** phones (telephone/satellite/cellular), carrier plans, and vehicle-mounted fans made **ineligible**; computing devices necessary to operate awarded equipment made **eligible**.
Sources: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-key-changes_05182026.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/assistance-grants/faqs

### 2b. Vehicle Acquisition
Funds fire apparatus, vehicles, and trailers. Eligible apparatus **include but are not limited to**: pumper/engine, aerial, quint, brush truck, tanker/tender, rescue vehicles, and ambulances.

- **Cost cap:** Not more than **25%** of available grant funds may be used for vehicle purchases.
- **Ambulance set-aside:** Of that vehicle funding, FEMA intends to dedicate **10%** to ambulances (~2.5% of total available grant funds).

Sources: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_fy2024_afg_nofo.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-factsheet_05182026.pdf

### 2c. Regional projects
Encourage collaboration between multiple organizations, funding initiatives that benefit a broad region rather than a single entity (e.g., shared training facilities, mutual-aid communications). Eligible Regional activities include **vehicle acquisition, professional training, and wellness/fitness** projects.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-factsheet_05182026.pdf

### 2d. Micro Grants (voluntary option)
Applicants requesting **$75,000 or less** in total AFG funds across all applications in a year are eligible for the voluntary Micro Grant option for eligible Operations & Safety activities. Micro Grant applications compete **within their own activity pool** rather than against the full field. Choosing Micro Grant for Operations & Safety does **not** affect the request or federal participation under Vehicle Acquisition or Regional programs.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/node/what-micro-grant ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_fy2024_afg_nofo.pdf

## 3. Key rules

### Cost share / match (by population served)
Based on census population of the served jurisdiction; must be a **cash match** (in-kind not allowable). Need not be in hand at application, but available when the application is accepted.

| Population served | Minimum non-federal cost share |
|---|---|
| 20,000 or fewer | 5% |
| More than 20,000 up to 1,000,000 | 10% |
| More than 1,000,000 | 15% |

Source: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd-fy23-afg-cost-share-calculator.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-nofo_05182026.pdf

### Maximum federal award (by population served)
- 100,000 or fewer: **$1 million** maximum per fiscal year.
- More than 100,000 up to 500,000: **$2 million** maximum.
- Higher tiers: higher caps apply, but exact upper-tier amounts are **not verified** from a FEMA source.

Source (lower tiers): https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-nofo_05182026.pdf

### Eligible applicants
- Fire departments (career, volunteer, and combination)
- Nonaffiliated EMS organizations
- State Fire Training Academies (SFTAs)

Must certify eligibility per the funding notice and maintain an **active SAM.gov registration** at application, throughout review, and at award.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-nofo_05182026.pdf ; https://sam.gov/fal/2b0b958335614ca591870cbbe0764c49/view

### Funding priorities
FEMA sets annual priorities and evaluation criteria on recommendations from the **Criteria Development Panel (CDP)**, a panel of fire-service professionals convened each year.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/assistance-grants

### Period of performance
**24 months (two years).**
Source: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-nofo_05182026.pdf

### Required application components (the narrative)
Six narrative sections; **four are peer-reviewed and scored, each worth 25% of the Peer Review Panel score:**
1. **Financial Need** — financial distress, budget constraints, unsuccessful other-funding attempts, distress beyond the applicant's control.
2. **Project Description** — objectives and relationship to budget/risk analysis; activities consistent with mission and national/state/local requirements.
3. **Cost/Benefit** — cost-effectiveness of the request.
4. **Statement of Effect** — impact of the project.

The peer-review score is averaged with an electronic pre-score for the final score. FEMA provides **Self-Evaluation Sheets** (Operations & Safety; Vehicle Acquisition), an **Application Checklist**, and a **Narrative Development Guide/Toolkit**.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_rsl_gpd_fy25-afg-narrative-development-guide_05182026.pdf

### Technical assistance
The **Fire Grants Program Help Desk** assists with application completion/submission in **FEMA Grants Outcomes (FEMA GO)** and answers eligibility/responsibility questions.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/assistance-grants/documents

## Funding history (context)
- Since 2001, AFG has awarded approximately **$8.7 billion** total.
- **FY 2024:** Congress appropriated **$324M**; **1,678 grants** awarded totaling **$291.6M**.
- **FY 2022:** **1,995 awards** totaling **$720M**.
- **FY 2020:** **$355M** appropriated to AFG.
- **FY 2025:** **$291.6M** available, ~1,800 anticipated awards.

A full year-by-year table was **not verified**; the OpenFEMA dataset is authoritative for award-level history.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/assistance-grants ; https://www.fema.gov/openfema-data-page/non-disaster-and-assistance-firefighter-grants-v1
