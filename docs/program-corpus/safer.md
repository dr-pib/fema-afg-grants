## 2. Eligible activities (what SAFER funds)

SAFER funds fire departments and volunteer-firefighter interest organizations to increase or maintain the number of trained front-line firefighters, to better meet NFPA staffing/response standards (NFPA 1710 career, NFPA 1720 volunteer).
Source: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/safer

### (a) Hiring of Firefighters (Hiring) Activity
- **Covers:** hiring new/additional firefighters, converting part-time/paid-on-call to full-time, rehiring laid-off firefighters, retaining firefighters facing imminent layoff. Three categories:
  - **New Hire** — new, additional firefighters.
  - **Rehire** — firefighters laid off within the two years prior to the application period start.
  - **Retention** — firefighters facing imminent layoff (within 120 days of the application close, or under a formal layoff notice issued before the period opened).
- **Eligible expenses:** ONLY salary + associated benefits (actual payroll) for funded positions. "Usual annual cost" of a first-year firefighter = base salary (excluding non-FLSA overtime) + standard benefits (health/dental/vision, FICA, life insurance, retirement/pension).
- **Who can apply:** career, combination, and volunteer fire departments; municipalities; fire districts. Volunteer/combination departments use Hiring to bring on career (paid) firefighters; a portion of Hiring funds is set aside for all-/majority-volunteer departments (search text cited 10% — **not verified against the NOFO**).
Sources: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd_safer-fact-sheet_fy24.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd-fy-2023-safer-faqs.pdf

### (b) Recruitment and Retention (R&R) of Volunteer Firefighters Activity
- **Covers:** recruiting/retaining volunteer firefighters. Example eligible activities: marketing/advertising, new-recruit basic training, leadership/career-development training, and PPE for new recruits.
- **Who can apply:** volunteer and combination fire departments; and national/regional/state/local/tribal/nonprofit interest organizations representing volunteer firefighters. **Career (all-paid) departments are NOT eligible for R&R.**
Sources: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/safer ; https://www.nvfc.org/safer-recruitment-and-retention-grant-tips/

## 3. Key rules

### Cost share / match (and waivers)
- **Statutory Hiring cost share:** federal funding may not exceed **75% of a first-year firefighter's usual annual cost in Years 1 and 2, and 35% in Year 3** (declining federal share).
- **R&R cost share:** **none.**
- **FY2023 waiver:** FEMA's FY2023 FAQ states **no cost share/match and no position cost limit** that year.
- **FY2024/FY2025 waiver status:** third-party grant services report FY2025 fully waives the local match; FEMA's FY2024 fact sheet still shows 75/75/35. **Genuine source conflict — treat the FY24/FY25 full waiver as reported-but-not-verified.**
- **Position cost limit:** search/CRS text cited $90,000 (Yr1), $90,000 (Yr2), $42,000 (Yr3) per position — **not verified.**
- **Hardship waiver:** FEMA may waive/reduce cost-share and/or maintenance-of-effort on a showing of economic hardship.
Sources: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd_safer-fact-sheet_fy24.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd-fy-2023-safer-faqs.pdf ; https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48283

### Eligibility (by activity)
- **Hiring:** career, combination, volunteer fire departments; municipalities; fire districts.
- **R&R:** volunteer and combination departments; plus national/regional/state/local/tribal/nonprofit interest organizations. Career departments not eligible for R&R.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/safer

### Period of performance
- **Hiring:** **36 months**, following a default **180-day recruitment period** starting at award approval (the 36-month clock starts automatically after 180 days). Positions must be retained for the full performance period.
- **R&R:** **12, 24, 36, or 48 months**, following a default **90-day recruitment period**.
Source: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd-fy-2023-safer-faqs.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/safer

### Required application components
- Submitted through **FEMA GO**.
- Standard forms: **SF-424** family (SF-424A/B assurances, SF-LLL lobbying disclosure).
- **Narrative responses** to each NOFO evaluation criterion (R&R is heavily narrative-weighted — search text cited ~70% of scoring).
- Operational data: prior-3-years incident/response counts (primary-response alarms only, excluding mutual aid given); frontline first-alarm vehicle inventory.
Sources: https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fy_2025_safer-application_checklist.pdf ; https://www.fema.gov/sites/default/files/documents/fema_gpd_safer-application-checklist_fy24.pdf

### Program context (funding history & timeline)
- **FY2024 & FY2025:** ~$324M to SAFER each year (FY2024's $648M appropriation split evenly AFG/SAFER).
- **FY2025 window:** opened Tue May 19, 2026 9am ET; closed Mon Jun 22, 2026 5pm ET; awards begin ~Sept 14, 2026.
- **Cumulative:** FY2017–FY2023, 2,822 grants totaling $3,384,880,288; since 2005, over $5.2B in fire-staffing grants.
- **Reauthorization:** Fire Grants and Safety Act (P.L. 118-67, signed Jul 9, 2024) reauthorized AFG and SAFER at $750M/program/year through FY2028.
Sources: https://www.fema.gov/grants/preparedness/firefighters/safer ; https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48283
